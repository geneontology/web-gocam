Causal Activity Models (CAMs) April 2018

GO-CAMs: linking standard GO Annotations

GO-Causal Activity Models (GO-CAMs) are a generalization of “standard” GO annotations (one gene product associated with one GO term). They use a defined “grammar” for linking multiple standard GO annotations into larger models of biological function (such as “pathways”) in a semantically structured manner. GO-CAMs are created by expert biocurators from the GO Consortium, using the Noctua modeling tool.

GO-CAMs: multiple uses

  • “reduced” to standard GO annotations (these are included in the standard GO annotation files).
  • As networks representing the causal interactions between the activities of different gene products (we provide Cytoscape Simple Interaction Format).
  • As full GO-CAM graphs that can be downloaded (as ttl) and queried via SPARQL.
  • As “pathway diagrams” that can be browsed and visualized.
